About Me

After graduating from the University of New South Wales (UNSW) with a background in Construction Management & Property, I transitioned into the world of technology through my graduate studies at Tsinghua University's Global Innovation eXchange (GIX) Institute. There, I developed a deep interest in the intersection of design, AIoT, and data systems, quickly building technical skills in Python and Java to apply in real-world projects from audio processing to computer vision.

One of my key projects involved building an intelligent shopping cart using visual recognition and RFID, designed to improve the retail experience. This experience ultimately led me to the University of Washington, where I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Technology Innovation. At UW, I continue to explore how human-centered design and emerging technologies can improve everyday life, blending creativity with technical precision in areas like embedded systems, responsible AI, and prototyping.
